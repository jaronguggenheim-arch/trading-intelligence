How the signals work

🏦
Insider Cluster Buys
up to +40 pts

What it is

When corporate insiders (executives, board members, 10% owners) buy shares with their own money and file a Form 4 with the SEC, it's public record. A single insider buy is moderately meaningful. A cluster — three or more buys from different executives in the same 30-day window — is one of the most reliably bullish signals in the market.

Insiders can sell for many reasons (tax, diversification, estate planning). But they buy for one reason: they believe the stock is going higher.

"3 ASML executives purchased a combined $2.4M in shares over 8 days — a rare cluster buy that historically precedes a 12–18% move over the following 60 days."
🌊
Dark Pool Prints
up to +30 pts

What it is

Dark pools are private, off-exchange trading venues where institutional investors execute large block trades without publicly impacting the market. These trades are reported to FINRA and become visible, just with a delay. A large block printed at or above the ask price signals institutional accumulation — someone wants in, and they're not waiting for a dip.

Dark pool data doesn't tell you why an institution is buying. But it tells you that a major player with serious research behind them made a big bet.

"$340M dark pool block on NVDA printed at $870 — above the current ask. Unusual size suggests an institution accumulated a new or expanded position."
⚡
Options Flow
up to +25 pts

What it is

Options sweep detection identifies large, aggressive options orders that sweep multiple exchanges — meaning the buyer needed to fill immediately regardless of price. Sweeps above a certain size relative to average daily volume, especially out-of-the-money calls with 30–60 day expiry, often front-run catalysts by days or weeks.

This doesn't mean the trade will always pay off. But when options flow aligns with insider buys and dark pool prints on the same ticker, the signal convergence is meaningful.

"$2.1M call sweep on PLTR, 45-day expiry, 12× average daily options volume. Multi-exchange sweep suggests urgency — someone expects a move before May 16th."
📊
Analyst Upgrades
up to +20 pts

What it is

Analyst revisions from Tier 1 institutions (Goldman Sachs, Morgan Stanley, JPMorgan, etc.) carry more weight than consensus moves, especially when a price target moves significantly above the current stock price. The signal value increases when multiple analysts move in the same direction within a short window — a revision cluster.

Analyst downgrades are weighted negatively in the score. A strong buy from Goldman after a period of muted analyst activity is one of the cleaner momentum triggers.

"Goldman Sachs upgraded ASML to Strong Buy, raising PT from $720 → $850. 5th analyst upgrade this quarter; consensus PT now $30 above current price."
🧮
GEX / Gamma Exposure
up to +20 pts

What it is

Gamma Exposure (GEX) measures how much market makers need to hedge their options books as price moves. When GEX flips from negative to positive, market makers switch from amplifying moves to dampening them — which creates a magnetic effect toward key levels. A positive GEX flip above a key technical level often precedes a squeeze or breakout.

GEX is a directional signal for short-term price behavior. We use it as a momentum confirmation layer rather than a primary signal.

"NVDA crossed into positive GEX at $880. Dealers are now net long gamma — they buy dips and sell rallies, which stabilizes price and can support a grind higher."
🔗
Supply Chain Correlation
up to +15 pts

What it is

Key nodes in a supply chain tend to move in correlated fashion, but with lag — when ASML (lithography) beats on orders, TSM (fabrication) typically follows 3–7 trading days later as the market reprices upstream demand. We map 6 supply chains and use historical lag data to score whether a chain move is likely to propagate downstream.

This signal adds conviction when combined with insider or options data on the downstream ticker.

"ASML reported record Q1 bookings. Based on historical correlation, TSM and NVDA have followed with 3–7 day lag in 7 of the last 9 instances."

🌿 Sage Mode: the 10-second morning ritual

All of the signals above feed into a 0–100 conviction score for each ticker in our watchlist. Sage Mode takes the ticker with the highest signal convergence each morning and presents it as a single decision card.

"Three ASML executives bought with their own money this week — that's rare and historically bullish. Goldman raised their price target to $850 this morning. Conviction score: 91. Do you want in?"

There are no charts to interpret. No data tables. No jargon. Just the signal, the reason it matters, and two buttons: I'm in or Skip today.

Sage is designed for the moments when you have 10 minutes before work and want to know: is there something worth acting on today? It doesn't replace deeper research — it surfaces the one opportunity most worth spending that research time on.

Think of it as a distillation, not a shortcut. The full signal stack is always accessible in the Deep Edge, Signal Scores, and Smart Money tabs. Sage just answers the first question: is today worth looking at ASML?
